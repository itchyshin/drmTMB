# After Task: Beta-Binomial `mu` Random Intercepts

## Goal

Add the denominator-aware paired slice for `beta_binomial()`: ordinary
unlabelled `mu` random intercepts with
`bf(cbind(success, failure) ~ x + (1 | id), sigma ~ z)` for counted successes
out of known trials.

## Implemented

`beta_binomial()` now accepts ordinary unlabelled location random intercepts in
the `mu` formula. The random intercept enters the logit success-probability
predictor, the response still uses two-column success/failure counts with
known trial totals, and `sigma` remains a fixed-effect log-scale formula for
extra-binomial variation.

The fitted random-effect SD is exposed through `sdpars$mu`, conditional
effects through `random_effects$mu`, direct interval targets through
`profile_targets()`, and first-pass replication diagnostics through
`check_drm()`.

## Mathematical Contract

For observation `i` in group `j`:

```text
logit(mu_ij) = X_mu[i, ] beta_mu + b_j
b_j = sd_mu u_j
u_j ~ Normal(0, 1)
log(sigma_i) = X_sigma[i, ] beta_sigma
phi_i = 1 / sigma_i^2
p_i ~ Beta(mu_i phi_i, (1 - mu_i) phi_i)
y_i | p_i, n_i ~ Binomial(n_i, p_i)
```

This contract does not open beta-binomial random slopes, labelled covariance
blocks, `sigma` random effects, exact 0/1 boundary mass, `zoi`/`coi`,
structured bounded-response effects, known covariance, or bivariate/mixed
bounded-response models.

## Tests Of The Tests

The new beta-binomial recovery test initially failed because `check_drm()` did
not route `beta_binomial()` through the random-effect replication diagnostics.
Adding `beta_binomial` to the shared diagnostic admission lists made the test
exercise the public diagnostic surface as well as likelihood fitting,
extractors, direct profile targets, and denominator-aware conditional
prediction.

The malformed-neighbour tests cover beta-binomial random slopes, labelled
covariance, `sigma` random effects, and planned `coi`/`zoi` paths.
